* Instruments (the majors)

The _instruments_ we will be focused on are the _Major Currency pairs,
or "Majors," are the most liquid, high-volume currency pairs in the
forex market, representing ~85% of total trading volume. They always
include the U.S. dollar (USD) paired with other major currencies,
offering low spreads, high liquidity, and consistent news coverage.

** EUR/USD ("The Fiber"): Most traded, low volatility.
** USD/JPY ("The Gopher/Ninja"): Often trades in trends, safe-haven status.
** GBP/USD ("The Cable"): Highly liquid, often more volatile than EUR/USD.
** USD/CHF ("The Swissy"): Paired with the Swiss Franc, a safe-haven currency.
** AUD/USD ("The Aussie"): Sensitive to commodity prices (e.g., gold).
** USD/CAD ("The Loonie"): Highly correlated with oil prices.
** NZD/USD ("The Kiwi"): Influenced by agricultural exports and tourism.
