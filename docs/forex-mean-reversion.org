* Overview

Mean reversion in forex is well-suited to swing trading timeframes and has a
decent empirical basis — currency pairs tend to revert to fair value faster
than equities because of the carry/interest rate anchor and the large,
competitive nature of the market.

* Strategies

** Pairs-Based Statistical Arbitrage

The most rigorous mean reversion approach. Find two currency pairs that are
*cointegrated* (not just correlated) — meaning their spread is stationary over
time.

*** Classic cointegrated pairs
- EURUSD / GBPUSD
- AUDUSD / NZDUSD
- USDCAD / USDCHF

*** Mechanics
- Go long the underperformer and short the overperformer when the spread
  deviates beyond a Z-score threshold (typically ±2σ)
- Exit when the spread reverts to zero

*** Caveats
- The cointegration relationship must be tested periodically (Engle-Granger
  or Johansen tests) — it can break down during regime shifts
- Treat cointegration as a time-limited, not permanent, assumption

** Bollinger Band Mean Reversion

Simple and often effective on H4/Daily timeframes for majors. Price deviates
more than 2σ from a 20-period moving average; fade the move — but /only/ with
a filter. The raw strategy is brutal in trending markets.

*** Required filters (pick at least one)
- *ADX filter*: skip trades when ADX(14) > 25 — trend is too strong
- *Volatility regime filter*: avoid when ATR percentile is spiking (signals
  breakout, not reversion)

** RSI Extremes on Higher Timeframes

RSI < 30 / > 70 on Daily or Weekly is a reasonable entry trigger for majors.

*** Best used as a confirmation filter, not standalone
- Wait for RSI to *exit* the extreme zone before entering
- Entering the moment RSI touches the extreme fights momentum directly

** Interest Rate / Fair Value Reversion

Longer-timeframe swing approach. Currencies that have diverged significantly
from PPP or interest rate differentials tend to revert over weeks to months.

*** Known anchors
| Pair      | Fundamental anchor               |
|-----------+----------------------------------|
| AUDUSD    | Iron ore / commodity terms       |
| USDCAD    | Crude oil                        |
| AUD/JPY   | Carry spread vs. risk sentiment  |

These are slower-moving but give you a fundamental anchor for the mean, which
makes entries more defensible than a purely statistical approach.

* Practical Principles

These distinguish strategies that hold up from those that don't.

** The mean needs a reason to exist

Bollinger Bands give a statistical mean with no fundamental anchor. A spread
between cointegrated pairs, or a deviation from a carry-implied rate, gives
the market a reason to pull back toward it.

** Session timing matters

Forex mean reversion performs better during the Asian/London overlap and
degrades around major news events. Gate entries on session time and avoid the
first 30 minutes of London/NY opens.

** The exit is as important as the entry

Mean reversion profits are bounded — you are not riding a trend. Exit at the
mean (20-period MA, or zero Z-score on the spread) rather than holding for a
full reversal.

** Account for correlated exposure in sizing

Trading EURUSD and GBPUSD as independent mean reversion signals simultaneously
doubles USD exposure. Either trade the spread directly, or adjust position
sizing for cross-instrument correlation (treat them as a correlated basket, not
two independent 0.5% risk trades).

* Fit for the Trader System

The cointegration pairs approach is the most interesting fit for the current
architecture.

** Why it maps well

- The S&R detection pipeline and candle infrastructure plug directly into a
  spread series — the spread is just another price series
- The per-instrument goroutine model in ~RunPortfolio~ could run a spread as a
  synthetic instrument with two legs (long leg + short leg)

** Open architectural question

Before the equities scanner work locks in the instrument abstraction layer, it
is worth deciding whether the ~Instrument~ type needs to represent *synthetic
instruments* (i.e., a spread between two underlyings rather than a single
tradeable asset). Deferring this decision risks the interface being too narrow
to accommodate pairs trading without a later breaking change.
